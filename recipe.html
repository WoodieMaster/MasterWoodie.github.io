Spare Ribs


Ingredients

  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup fajita seasoning (such as Fiesta®)
  • 2 tablespoons Hungarian sweet paprika
  • 2 racks pork spareribs, fat trimmed
  • 1 cup beer
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 3 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon prepared brown mustard

Directions

  1. Mix brown sugar, fajita seasoning, and paprika in a bowl.
  2. Rub both sides of pork spareribs with the brown sugar mixture.
  3. Place spareribs in a 9x13-inch baking pan; cover and refrigerate 8 hours or overnight.
  4. Preheat the oven to 250 degrees F (120 degrees C).
  5. Whisk together beer, garlic, honey, Worcestershire sauce, and mustard in a bowl; Set that aside
  6. Tear off 2 large sheets of heavy-duty aluminum foil and lay them shiny-side down.
  7. Place a rack of spare ribs on each sheet, meaty-side up.
  8. Tear off 2 more sheets of foil and place them on top of the spare ribs, shiny-side up.
  9. Begin tightly folding the edges of the foil together to create a sealed packet.
  10. Just before sealing completely, divide beer mixture evenly into each packet.
  11. Complete the seal.
  12. Place the packets side-by-side on an 11x14-inch baking sheet.
  13. Bake ribs in the preheated oven until the ribs are very tender, 3 1/2 to 4 hours.
  14. Carefully open each packet and drain drippings into a saucepan.
  15. You may only need the drippings from one packet.
  16. Place ribs on a foil-lined baking sheet and set aside.
  17. Heat the saucepan over medium-high and simmer drippings until the sauce begins to thicken, about 5 minutes.
  18. Brush the thickened sauce over ribs.
  19. Preheat the broiler and set the oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source.
  20. Return ribs to the oven and broil until the sauce is lightly caramelized, 5 to 7 minutes.
